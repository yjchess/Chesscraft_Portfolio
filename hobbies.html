Hobbies

Despite Chesscraft's numerous exploits and highly ambitiious goals, he is still human. And as a human, he still has his own interests, likes and wants separate to those of his goals. Whilst it is possible for these hobbies to develop into something more serious, for the moment - they remain hobbies. Purely activities Chesscraft does for fun. Although that is not to say that Chesscraft does not give them his full attention when doing these activities. On the contrary, Chesscraft will strive to do the best in everything that he does.

Main Categories

The hobbies of Chesscraft can be split into three categories:

Throughout Chesscraft's life, video games has always been a constant in his life. In fact, it is arguable that video games are the saving grace that helped Chesscraft survive and become the man he is today. They have been such a vital part of his life and have probably saved his life on more than one occasion.

RTS Games

Real Time Strategy games have been a core element in Chesscraft's life - since a very young age. Games such as Starcraft 1, Age of Empires 2 and Warcraft III defined his childhood.


Even now - RTS games are his favourite genre and he still thoroughly enjoys Starcraft II as well as IMMORTAL: Gates of Pyre. He has also experimented with countless other RTS games such as: Warcraft: Orcs and Humans.Chesscraft has played around with the idea of becoming a pro - it seems unlikely as of now, however, he does not wish to shut any doors just yet.


"The beauty of RTS games lies in the micro and macromanagement of units and buildings, to me - the game is more than just pixels dancing on a screen - to me, it is the complete and utter expression of one's self." - Chesscraft 16/01/2021

RPG Games

Chesscraft has always enjoyed more story driven games. However, he plays these more as a means of relaxation than anything serious.


In earlier years, Chesscraft's favourite RPG would have been either AdventureQuest; a cute adventure game with cool looking characters and a heavily story-driven plot with multiple optional sidequests or Diablo II.


He also used to love playing MMORPGS, and fell in love with them with the rise of World Of Warcraft, which he would play at his friend's house every now and then in his youth. As he grew up - he lost access to WoW, but began playing free mmorpgs like Guild Wars 2 which he enjoyed.


Nowadays, Chesscraft plays fewer RPGs, but will do so every now and again.

Rhythym Games

Rhythym Games have been a somewhat recent addition to Chesscraft's repertoire of games.


Initially, Chesscraft began playing Rhythym Games as a way to practice his mouse accuracy and precision. However, over time - he began to love the challenge that came with rhythym games.


Chesscraft loves skill-based games, especially ones where you can feel your own improvement and see the results almost immediately.


Recently, Chesscraft has picked up playing Beat Saber - a VR Rhythym game that requires you to move around and hit blocks with light sabers in a specific direction and sequence. He currently uses this as exercise and loves the game.

Platformer Games

In many ways Platformer Games are in a similar vein to Rhythym Games.


Platformers are skill based games dependent on timing and accuracy (very similar to Rhythym games). However - they are vastly different in that they use controls as opposed to mouse accuracy, and although you often have to enter a precise sequence of moves - there are often multiple answers to a single stage.


Chesscraft Began playing platformers more recently (although he did play Mario games briefly in his youth at a friend's house). He has received a few games from friends (some of which are temporary) and has been busy playing games like: Ori, Hollow Knight and Celeste.

MOBA Games

Chesscraft used to love MOBA games dearly. Specifically he would play League of Legends. He used to love that game throughout Secondary school.


Chesscraft loved the skill based nature of MOBA games, and League was the first game to introduce him to the concept of Esports (despite playing SC2). He supported TSM and thoroughly appreciated the beauty of mechanical plays like MadLife's Thresh Hooks.


However, over time - Chesscraft's enthusiasm for MOBA's waned due to the toxic nature of communities. Thus he transitioned to RTS Games due to there being only one player per team. And more often than not - the most critical person of your mistakes is yourself

Other Games

Sometimes, when Chesscraft wants to fully relax, he may pick a different game with either a lower level of skill required or one which he does not care about as much in terms of seriousness.


Chesscraft will instead cruise through these games, focussing fully on his enjoyment of the game and the experiences that it provides him. These games can come in all forms and sizes, like a game to find hidden items - a mobile Idle app or a pet raising game.


It is interesting that there are games in this section which do have an extremely high level cap like fighting games, however - Chesscraft decides to play these games at a more leisurely rate - preferring instead to enjoy the experience than worry about the destination.

RTS Games

It is no understatement to say that RTS Games have perhaps been some of the most influential games in my life. My Journey in RTS games begins with the nostalgic scenes of childhood, wherein my brother would share his usbs or dvds with me so I could install and play Starcraft 1, followed shortly by Age of Empires III. I can remember vividly now when I would battle against my brother and he would allow me to build a mass of units, before suddenly dismantling me with his array of stealthed units.

The strategies in RTS games are limitless and creativity can flow free. The possible moves and required mechanics to play perfectly are nigh infinite - hence there are always improvements to be had, and no two games are the same. It is because of this, that I feel like I can fully express myself through Real Time Strategy Games. Each tiny movement, each building, unit and more were planned directly by me and are an extension of my will made into code.

When Starcraft II became free to play - I jumped with joy, and it became one of my goals to become recognized and adored in the SCII community. I came close a few times, with my appearances on famous Youtuber channels like: WinterStarcraft, X5_Pig and Florencio, however - I never really made it far enough. My most famous appearances are on a series called Bronze League Heroes - by Winterstarcraft, wherein he casts players with unique styles of play. For me, this was a focus on micro, the fact that I would try to never give up and the overbuilding of workers.

Unfortunately, due to recent events - Chesscraft is making preparations for the transition from SC2 to a different RTS. This is due to the changes announced at Blizzard. He currently is supporting an up and coming game called: IMMORTAL: Gates of Pyre.

Writing, Reading, Art, Creating and more!

Chesscraft has always had a flair for creativity since a very young age. Whilst at first his ambitions pointed him towards the idea of becoming an inventor, he slowly discovered other methods of expressing himself creatively.

Chesscraft's life of reading began from a very young age. Given his brother's books, he began to explore the wildly imaginative worlds of Roald Dahl and C.S. Lewis. Perhaps it was these books which caused Chesscraft to have such interest in the strange and unique, the colourful and unexplored.

At 6 years of age, Chesscraft's brother gave to him his first Harry Potter Novel; Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone. It is interesting that at first Chesscraft was mostly disinterested in the book - feeling bored after having read only the first few pages about a cat. However, after being encouraged to give it another go, he became heavily invested in the world of Harry Potter.

However, Literature is but one method through which creativity may be expressed. Whilst words may paint a picturesque scene in one's mind - it is often said that: "a picture paints a thousand words". From the very start, Chesscraft has always loved artistry. He enjoyed drawing with a pencil most of all, and whilst other kids his age would hate the rain during break - he always looked forward to rainy days, because he would be allowed to draw inside.

Chesscraft always enjoyed the sensation of drawing freely, to let the art flow onto the page as his imagination ran wild. However, that being said - he always preferred working with pencil and paper from a young age due to the ease of erasing out mistakes.

One of the most memorable pieces that Chesscraft created was an abstract painting that he painted. His teacher hung the piece on the wall and when asked by the other students why a year 7's artwork was up on the wall with all the sixth former's works he replied that he loved the piece because of it's duality; the way it was both so simple and yet so complicated. The piece had many interpretations, it could be something as miniscule as an atom or as large as the start of the universe. I would love to show you the piece, but unfortunately it is no longer in my posession.

Chesscraft has since transitioned to more digital art like digital sculpting and 2d digital art. Unfortunately due to the mental wall he experiences due to executive dysfunction he no longer draws as much as he used to in his youth, but is working on building that back up.

On top of Literature and Art, Chesscraft also loves to create/edit objects in real life. Whether this may be from folding Origami, to assembling and painting miniatures - Chesscraft loves this kind of hobby. He has always taken a liking towards creating physical objects with love and detail.

Some of these projects like Origami and Paper Toys were only a part of his life briefly, but yet - he still loved them all. Other hobbies, like his growing Warhammer 40K and Age of Sigmar model collection are still present in his life. Chesscraft has always had a fancy for detailed models and figures. He is also an avid collector (albeit a broke collector who relies more on gifts than buying his own) and has collected multiple gogos, figures and other toys.

Recently Chesscraft has discovered a new hobby: crocheting. It is a relatively cheap hobby, which can produce some of the most adorable toys and is challenging enough to be fun.
